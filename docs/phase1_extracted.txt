[1]  Implementation  of  Secure  E2E  Chat  App  Using  DH  and  AES-256  (MDPI,  2025)  
Propose  a  secure  chat  architecture  within  a  microservice  environment.  
Used  Diffie-Hellman  (DH)  for  key  exchange  and  AES-256  for  symmetric  encryption.  
Confirmed  high  throughput  and  low  latency  for  secure  communication  in  microservices.  
Standard  DH  is  vulnerable  to  "Harvest  Now,  Decrypt  Later"  quantum  threats.  
[2]  End-to-End  Encrypted  Messaging  Protocols:  An  Overview  (Ermoshina  et  al.,  2016)  
Survey  and  analyze  core  protocols  used  in  decentralized  and  E2E  messaging.  
Comparative  survey  of  30  projects  including  Signal,  PGP,  and  various  email-oriented  services.  
Provided  a  foundational  taxonomy  of  decentralized  vs.  centralized  E2EE  protocols.  
As  a  2016  paper,  it  lacks  coverage  of  modern  Post-Quantum  (PQ)  algorithm  transitions.  

[3]  End-to-End  Encryption:  Evolution,  Barriers,  and  Emerging  Trends  (Springer,  2025)  
Examine  multidisciplinary  barriers  and  trends  in  modern  E2EE  deployments.  
Case  study  analysis  of  platforms  like  WhatsApp  using  AES,  RSA,  and  DH.  
Industry  standards  are  well-defined,  but  research  gaps  exist  in  automated  user  authentication.  
Identifies  a  lack  of  security  in  automated  authentication  and  audio-specific  channels.  
[4]  Advancements  and  Comparative  Analysis  of  Digital  Signature  Algorithms  (IJRASET,  2023)  
Compare  security  properties  of  various  digital  signature  algorithms.  
Reviewed  RSA  variants  (Batch,  MultiPrime)  and  Elliptic  Curve  (ECDSA)  algorithms.  
ECDSA  offers  better  performance  than  RSA  variants;  highlighted  resistance  to  chosen-message  attacks.  
High  computational  overhead  for  some  MultiPrime  RSA  variants  in  resource-constrained  environments.  
[5]  Encryption  Techniques  for  Different  Messenger  Applications  (IJITEE,  2019)  
Benchmark  encryption  standards  across  popular  consumer  messaging  apps.  
Comparative  study  of  WhatsApp  (Curve25519)  vs.  other  apps  using  RSA/AES.  
Confirmed  AES-256  with  Curve25519  as  the  industry  standard  for  mobile  performance.  
Does  not  address  the  transition  to  Post-Quantum  algorithms  or  gRPC-based  transport.
